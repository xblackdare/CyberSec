Do I have cyber security?

Anti virus and me

I see you, mr "I have windows defender turned on meaning I'm invincible, I've turned off the PC scan feature because it slows me down sometimes and I..." NO! you aren't safe it's not as easy as that, for as much as I wish it was, but cyber attacks happen all the time, the truth is, it's complicated, obviously home users are not exactly some multi-billion pound industry there's always the chance that years can pass and nothing will happen but the day it does is the day you will wish you had some form of reliable virus protection, the saying goes that the best form of anti-virus is simply "common sense," don't download files that you think could be malicious or you don't know where they came from, don't give access to your accounts to people you don't know but sometimes things happen, sometimes you allow your 8 year old nephew on your PC and he decides to download some free RAM, sometimes you really trust your girlfriend so you give her your facebook password then she breaks up with you, you need to be prepared, have your anti-virus system (whatever it is) run regular scans, actually have a anti-virus system, use secure passwords, possibly a password you yourself don't even know (using a program such as dashlane) or if you do know your passwords, keep them unique and change them often, none of this "Password1" turning into "Password2" malarky
